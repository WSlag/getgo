## ARTICLE IV: TRUCKER'S OBLIGATIONS

The Trucker agrees to:

### 4.1 Transportation Duties
1. Transport the cargo from origin to destination using the designated vehicle
2. Exercise **extraordinary diligence** in handling the cargo as required by Philippine Civil Code for common carriers
3. Ensure cargo is properly secured during transit
4. Maintain communication with Shipper regarding shipment status
5. Deliver cargo to the designated consignee or authorized representative

### 4.2 Compliance Requirements
1. Maintain valid LTFRB Provisional Authority (PA) or Certificate of Public Convenience (CPC)
2. Possess valid LTO registration (OR/CR) for the vehicle
3. Hold a valid professional driver's license
4. Comply with all traffic laws and regulations
5. Maintain adequate rest periods to ensure safe driving

### 4.3 Prohibited Actions
The Trucker shall NOT:
1. Sub-contract or assign this transportation to another party without Shipper's written consent
2. Open, tamper with, or inspect the cargo contents
3. Deviate from the agreed route without valid reason
4. Abandon the cargo at any point during transit
5. Use the cargo for any unauthorized purpose

## ARTICLE V: SHIPPER'S OBLIGATIONS

The Shipper agrees to:

### 5.1 Cargo Preparation
1. Provide accurate description of cargo type, weight, and value
2. Properly package cargo suitable for transportation
3. Declare any hazardous, fragile, or special-handling items
4. Ensure cargo is legal and does not contain prohibited items
5. Provide all necessary documentation for the cargo

### 5.2 Coordination Duties
1. Be present or have authorized representative at pickup location
2. Ensure cargo is ready for loading at the agreed pickup time
3. Provide accurate delivery address and contact information
4. Confirm receipt of cargo upon delivery
5. Pay the agreed freight amount directly to the Trucker through the agreed payment method

### 5.3 Prohibited Actions
The Shipper shall NOT:
1. Include illegal, contraband, or prohibited items in the cargo
2. Misrepresent cargo contents, weight, or value
3. Refuse acceptance of cargo without valid reason
4. Make false claims against the Trucker

## ARTICLE VI: LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

### 6.1 Trucker's Liability
The Trucker shall be liable for:
1. **Scope of Custody**: Cargo loss, shortage, or damage occurring from pickup turnover (signed waybill/hand-over) until confirmed delivery
2. **Loss or Damage**: Direct and documented physical loss, shortage, or damage while cargo is in Trucker's custody, subject to the liability cap in Clause 6.3
3. **Delay**: Reasonable direct damages resulting from unreasonable delay, unless caused by force majeure
4. **Negligence**: Any damage resulting from Trucker's negligence or willful misconduct

### 6.2 Liability Exclusions
The Trucker shall NOT be liable for loss, damage, or delay solely caused by:
1. **Force Majeure**: Natural disasters (typhoon, earthquake, flood), war, civil unrest, government action
2. **Shipper's Fault**: Improper packaging, inaccurate declaration, failure to disclose hazardous nature
3. **Inherent Defect**: Natural deterioration, inherent vice, or defect in the cargo
4. **Public Authority**: Seizure or detention by government authorities due to Shipper's cargo

### 6.3 Maximum Liability Cap
1. **Declared Value Rule**: The Trucker's maximum liability for loss/damage is limited to the **Declared Value** stated in Article II.
2. **Undeclared Value Rule**: If no value is declared, the default maximum liability is **PHP 100,000**.
3. **Higher-Value Cargo**: For cargo exceeding PHP 100,000, Shipper is encouraged to declare full value before pickup and consider appropriate cargo insurance.
4. **Acknowledgment**: By signing this Contract, both parties acknowledge and accept this liability framework.

### 6.4 Cap Does Not Apply (Unlimited Liability Scenarios)
The liability cap in Clause 6.3 may not apply in cases of:
1. **Gross Negligence**
2. **Willful Misconduct**
3. **Fraud or Theft/Pilferage**
4. **Illegal Acts**

### 6.5 Claims Procedure
1. **Visible Damage**: Report immediately upon delivery, note on proof of delivery
2. **Hidden Damage**: Report within 48 hours of delivery with photographic evidence
3. **Filing Deadline**: Formal claims must be filed within 15 days of delivery
4. **Documentation Required**: Photos, receipts, proof of value, delivery documents

### 6.6 Shipper's Indemnification
The Shipper shall indemnify and hold harmless the Trucker from any claims arising from:
1. Inaccurate cargo declaration
2. Illegal or prohibited contents
3. Third-party claims related to the cargo
4. Shipper's breach of this Contract

## ARTICLE VIII: DISPUTE RESOLUTION

### 8.1 Good Faith Negotiation
In case of any dispute, the parties shall first attempt to resolve the matter through good faith negotiation within **seven (7) days**.

### 8.2 Mediation
If negotiation fails, either party may request mediation through:
1. Karga's dispute resolution service (optional, non-binding)
2. A mutually agreed mediator
3. The Office for Alternative Dispute Resolution (OADR)

Mediation shall be completed within **fourteen (14) days** of request.

### 8.3 Arbitration
If mediation fails, the dispute shall be resolved through binding arbitration in accordance with the **Alternative Dispute Resolution Act of 2004 (RA 9285)**:
1. Arbitration shall be conducted in [CITY], Philippines
2. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding
3. Each party shall bear their own costs, unless the arbitrator decides otherwise

### 8.4 Court Jurisdiction
For matters not subject to arbitration or for enforcement of arbitral awards, the courts of [CITY], Philippines shall have exclusive jurisdiction.
